underdetermination or the underdetermination of theory by data (sometimes abbreviated UTD) is the idea that evidence available to us at a given time may be insufficient to determine what beliefs we should hold in response to it.[1] The underdetermination thesis says that all evidence necessarily underdetermines any scientific theory.[2]
